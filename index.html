Who am I?

Hello, my name is Vishal Radhakrishnan, and I am glad to be having you read my portfolio! I am a second-year Mechanical Engineering Student at the University of Toronto and I transferred from Track One (General) Engineering from my 1st year. My interest in the amalgamation of electronics and structural physics helped me narrow down my choice to Mechanical Engineering as my core discipline. I believe some of my positives traits are being creative, having an urge to learn, and having a strong work ethic. Pursuing Mechanical engineering helps me align my interests and my skills in the best possible way for an exciting future career. My decision of selecting Mechanical Engineering was also driven by my interest in the Aerospace industry. However, I plan to have a broad mind towards my academics as of now, and seek something which piques my interest the most in the future!

My Vision

Science and Technology have never failed to excite me. By drastically changing our means of communication, the way we work, our housing, clothes, and food, our methods of transportation, and, indeed, even the length and quality of life itself, Science and Technology have generated changes in the moral values and basic philosophies of mankind. Considering the impact which science has on the world, I aspire to use my knowledge to contribute to society. Through this portfolio, I intend to present my abilities as an engineer, which will enable me to do so.

Design, Individual & Teamwork

My understanding of the “design process” is an approach to break down a big goal into smaller definable ones that combine to serve the required purpose. To me, the word “design” means the process to reach a final deliverable after iterating through a series of steps. The following 'Design Experiences' section describes my learning experiences through the various design projects I was a part of. A majority of these projects were possible as an outcome of collective thinking. We may have an individual design idea for a given project, but the idea needs validation for its use and iteration of steps for its enhancement. View my related thoughts and experiences on teamwork in the 'TeamWork Dreamwork' section of this website!
