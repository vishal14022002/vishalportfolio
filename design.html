Beginnings

Thinking back in time, designing has always been a part of my life. Unknowingly, I was introduced to designing as an infant in the process of stacking up lego blocks. It is extremely fascinating for me to think about how my skills of visualizing, innovating, and problem-solving have developed throughout my life. I believe my "actual" journey with design started as I became an Engineering student at the University of Toronto (in the year 2019). The word "actual" emphasizes the stage of my life when I started to understand the design process in more depth and detail. The following sections describe some of my significant design experiences and portray some highlights of these experiences.

My First Few design projects

Engineering Strategies and Practices(ESP) I and II (a sequence of courses taught during my first year) provided me with an insight into the engineering design process. Students in this course were required to do engineering projects in teams, while a professional procedure to do the projects was taught simultaneously. The projects were about developing viable solutions to address various real-life problems.

In ESP I, my group took on the project of devising design methods for the prevention of impact on crawl spaces during the time of flooding in the Toronto Islands. Road to Expertise

From the previous experiences discussed above, I could acquire an foundational understanding of the design process. It was time to switch gears!

This summer (2020), I introduced myself to a technical form of design- Web Design. This phase was a transformation for me from knowing and understanding the design process (planning candidate designs) to the implementation of my design ideas (by prototyping and testing). My portfolio is one of the outcomes of my use of this form of the design process!
